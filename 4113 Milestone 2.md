### Research

Opal Card System

The Transport for NSW Opal system is a public transport payment system used in Australis for buses,trains ,,ferries , and light rall services. It automatically calculates travel fares, applies daily travel caps, and stores journey history for passengers. The system also supports discounted fares for eligible passenger groups such as students and senior citizens.(Transport for NSW, n.d.).

What the system does well

- Automatically calculates transport fares

- Applies daily fare limits

- Supports passenger discounts

- Keeps records of journeys and travel history

Weakness

- Fare rules and pricing may sometimes be difficult for new users to understand

Features adapted for City Ride Lite

- Automatic fare calculation

- Daily fare cap system

- Passenger discount categories

- Journey tracking and summary reports

### Diary Entry 1 _06/05/26

For this part of Milestone 2, I focused on creating the flowcharts for the City Ride Lite program. Before starting the diagrams, I reviewed the requirements and ideas from Milestone 1 so that the system design would match the functions that need to be included in the final program.

I created a main system flowchart to show how the program moves between menu options and how different features connect together. The flowchart included processes such as adding journeys, validating user input, calculating fares, applying discounts and daily caps, viewing summaries, and resetting the system. I also added error handling steps to make sure the program can respond properly when invalid input is entered by the user.

The diagrams were designed independently using Miro. During the process, I adjusted the layout several times to improve the flow and make the structure easier to understand. Standard flowchart symbols were used throughout the design to clearly separate processes, decisions, inputs, and outputs.

Some additional guidance and clarification on flowchart structure and algorithm organisation were supported using ChatGPT, especially when checking whether the correct symbols and program flow were being used (OpenAI, 2025).

### Diary Entry 2 __07/05/26

This stage of Milestone 2 focused on researching existing public transport systems that have similar features to the City Ride Lite application. The aim of the research was to understand how real transport systems manage fare calculations, passenger discounts, journey records, and travel summaries.

During the research process, I investigated the Transport for London Oyster system and the Opal transport system from New South Wales. Both systems use automatic fare calculations and apply discounts and travel caps for passengers. I also explored how these systems organise information and display transport options to users. Screenshots of the interfaces were collected and included as supporting evidence in the report.

The research helped me identify useful ideas that could be adapted into my own program design, including journey tracking, fare calculation methods.
